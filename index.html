About me

I am currently a Post Doctoral Research associate at the University of Cambridge, where I conduct field work in Iceland as part of the Volcano-Seismology group. My research interests involve using ambient noise interferometry and surface wave tomography to determine the seismic velocity structure of volcanic regions, and improve the understanding of the dynamics in active volcanic systems. I also analyse micro-seismicity to understand magma migration in the subsurface, and use coulomb stress analysis to link magmatic processes to the triggering of earthquakes.
